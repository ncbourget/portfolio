About

Nate Bourget is a designer and creative technologist making identities, interfaces, publications, and visual systems for artists, institutions, and independent projects. The work moves between strategy and production, shaping ideas through careful typography, image, motion, and space.

  1. the studio — a design practice for cultural, editorial, and digital work with an emphasis on clear systems and expressive detail.
  2. the research — self-directed studies, experiments, and small editions that test how design can hold memory, context, and feeling.

These parts overlap. Nate works with collaborators to build visual languages for print, screen, and physical environments, making projects that feel precise without becoming cold.

A portion of the practice is reserved for mutual aid, civic, and community-led efforts. For commissions, collaborations, or notes, feel free to reach out.

Selected Work

The index gathers recent identity, editorial, web, installation, and motion projects. It is meant to work less like a gallery wall and more like a table of contents: compact, direct, and easy to move through.

Placeholder images stand in for project documentation while the structure settles. The rhythm is intentionally spare, giving each project room to be replaced by photographs, scans, or screen captures later.

Studies & Editions

Ongoing studies include small printed objects, typographic tests, interface sketches, and research fragments. These pieces are treated as finished thinking rather than leftovers from client work.

The format keeps the emphasis on process, materials, and usefulness: what a project notices, what it organizes, and what it makes easier to understand.

Out in the World

Work has appeared across exhibitions, small publications, independent websites, printed matter, and public-facing design systems. Collaborators include artists, organizers, educators, writers, and teams building things with a strong point of view.

The practice is especially interested in projects where the form can do more than decorate the content: clarify a structure, shift a reading, or make a body of work easier to share.

Collaborations

Past and current collaborators include studios, artists, organizers, publications, cultural spaces, and small teams with specific problems to solve. The best work tends to begin with a generous conversation and a clear constraint.

Colophon

This site is typeset with the local typefaces already included in the project and arranged as a quiet, text-forward index. Placeholder imagery can be swapped for project documentation as the archive grows.
